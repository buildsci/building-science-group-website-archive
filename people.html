About

The Building Science Group started in 2000 under the the leadership of Dr. Jelena Srebric to become the intersection of many collaborative research projects. It has since grown to include a diverse group of faculty, graduate, and undergraduate students across a number of universities and organizations. Members of the research group are from a variety of backgrounds including: mechanical engineering, architectural engineering, computer science, and public health.

Recent Events

Dr. Heidarinejad Selected For CPS Week 2015 Workshop

03/09/2015: Dr. Mohammad Heidarinejad was selected to present as part of a Microsoft SmartCity workshop at the 2015 Cypber-Physical Systems Week in Seattle, Washington from April 13-14. Dr. Heidarinejad was awarded a full travel grant to attend the event and present on the "Implication of Multi-Scale Modeling of Energy and Airflows in the Built Environment on the Design of Sustainable Smart Cities."

Matthew Dahlhausen Awarded ASHRAE Grant

03/02/2015: For the second time, Matthew Dahlhausen, a Doctoral candidate with CITY@UMD, has been awarded an ASHRAE Graduate Student Grant-In-Aid. Matthew was awarded the grant for his ongoing work on "Campus energy model calibration for metering and retrofit measure prioritization.”

CITY @ UMD Members Hold Spring Strategy Session

01/21/2015: Under the direction of Dr. Srebric, CITY@UMD memebers sat down and planed out the coming Spring Semester. Discussed topics include: publications, Virtual PULSE, collaborative work with UMD Facilities Management, in addition to personal goals for the upcoming semester. In attendence were: Nicholas Mattise, Matthew Dahlhausen, Dr. Yang-Seon Kim, Dr. Mohammad Heidarinejad, Dr. Jelena Srebric, Saber Koshdel-Nikkho; along with undergraduates: Krishang Sharma, Mike, and Itohan.
